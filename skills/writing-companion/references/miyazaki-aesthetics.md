# Miyazaki Aesthetics Reference

## Core Themes

### Wind and Movement
- Wind as character, not just weather
- Hair/clothing moving with air currents
- Kinetic energy of the natural world

### Transformation
- Characters becoming other forms (pigs, spirits, old age)
- Not as punishment, but as revelation
- Identity as fluid, not fixed

### Flight
- Literal (broomsticks, gliders, airships)
- Metaphorical (freedom, escape, perspective shift)
- Joy in defying gravity

### The Spirit World
- Parallel to human world, not separate
- Spirits have their own logic, not good/evil
- Respect for the unseen

### Anti-Villain
- Antagonists have understandable motivations
- No pure evil (even Yubaba loves her baby)
- Complexity over simplicity

### Childhood vs Adulthood
- Children see what adults miss
- Growing up as loss but also gain
- Wisdom in both states

## Visual Language

### Attention to Small Details
- Food preparation (bacon sizzling, rice steaming)
- Mechanical intricacy (howls castle, airship engines)
- Natural decay (rust, moss, weathering)

### Color Palette
- Natural greens, blues, earth tones
- Sudden saturation for emotional moments
- Sky as emotional state

### Pacing
- Long sequences of just movement (train ride in Spirited Away)
- Silence as important as sound
- No rush to get to plot

## Applying to Writing

### When Stuck for a Metaphor
Ask: "What would this look like in a Miyazaki film?"

- Technical debugging at 2 AM → Howl's room, chaos of books and spells
- Parenting → Kiki's Delivery Service, the baker's wife as mentor
- Career anxiety → Whisper of the Heart, the violin maker's workshop
- Finding balance → Ponyo, the sea and the shore

### Tone to Emulate
- Wonder without naivety
- Acceptance of imperfection
- Beauty in mundane moments
- Quiet determination over heroic gestures

### Phrases to Echo
- "There's something I need to do." (purpose without grandiosity)
- "I'm not afraid." (not bravado, but calm resolve)
- "Let's go." (simple, decisive)
